SIX SIGMA

Six Sigma is a quality management methodology that aims to improve the quality of a product or service by reducing defects and minimizing variability in the process. It is a data-driven approach that uses statistical tools and methodologies to identify and eliminate the causes of defects and errors. The name "Six Sigma" comes from the statistical measurement of a process's performance, which refers to the ability of a process to produce no more than 3.4 defects per million opportunities.

The Six Sigma methodology is based on the DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) process, which is a five-step approach to problem-solving. DMAIC process is used to improve an existing process, and DMADV (Define, Measure, Analyze, Design, Verify) process is used to create a new process.v

Six Sigma is widely used in manufacturing and service industries, including healthcare, finance, and government. It is particularly well-suited to industries that have high volumes of production or transactions and require a high degree of consistency and quality. By implementing Six Sigma, organizations can achieve significant improvements in quality, efficiency, and customer satisfaction, as well as cost savings.
