SA 8000

SA 8000 (Social Accountability 8000) is an international standard for social accountability and labor practices. It provides a framework for organizations to systematically manage their social and labor practices and comply with relevant laws and regulations. It was developed by Social Accountability International (SAI) and it covers areas such as child labor, forced labor, discrimination, disciplinary practices, working hours, compensation, and health and safety. Organizations that meet the requirements of SA 8000 can be certified to the standard, which demonstrates their commitment to social responsibility and ethical labor practices. SA 8000 is recognized globally and is accepted by major retailers and suppliers worldwide. The certification process is carried out by independent third-party certification bodies, that ensure the company is following the standard requirements and implementing the best practices.
